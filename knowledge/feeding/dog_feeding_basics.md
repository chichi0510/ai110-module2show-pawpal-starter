# Dog feeding basics

A consistent feeding schedule is one of the simplest ways an owner can support
a dog's digestion, weight, and behaviour. The exact amount depends on the
dog's life stage, breed size, body condition, and activity level, but the
patterns below are widely recommended.

## How often to feed

- **Puppies (under 6 months)**: 3–4 small meals per day. Their stomachs are
  small and their energy needs are high relative to body weight.
- **Puppies (6–12 months)**: transition to 2 meals per day.
- **Adult dogs (1+ year)**: 2 meals per day, roughly 12 hours apart, suits
  most healthy dogs better than a single large meal.
- **Senior dogs (7+ years for medium breeds, sooner for large breeds)**: keep
  twice-daily feeding but watch portion size — activity drops with age.

## How much to feed

Always start from the food label's guideline for the dog's weight, then
adjust by **body condition**:

- You should be able to feel ribs easily without seeing them.
- A waist should be visible when looking down from above.
- If the dog is gaining weight, reduce by ~10% and re-check in two weeks.

## What to feed

- A complete-and-balanced commercial diet labelled for the dog's life stage
  is the simplest safe choice.
- Fresh water must be available at all times.
- Treats should not exceed about 10% of daily calories.

## What to avoid

- Frequent table scraps, which add hidden calories and may include unsafe
  ingredients (see the toxic-food list).
- Sudden diet changes — transition over 7–10 days by mixing old and new food.
- Free-feeding (leaving food out all day) for most adult dogs, as it makes
  weight harder to manage and can mask a loss of appetite that signals
  illness.

## When to call a vet

- A dog refusing food for more than 24 hours.
- Repeated vomiting or diarrhoea after meals.
- Sudden weight loss or gain that does not match a portion change.
