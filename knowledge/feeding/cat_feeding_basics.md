# Cat feeding basics

Cats are obligate carnivores: their bodies are designed to get most of their
energy and nutrients from animal protein. A consistent, species-appropriate
feeding routine reduces obesity risk and helps spot health changes early.

## How often to feed

- **Kittens (under 6 months)**: 3–4 small meals per day. Energy needs are
  very high during rapid growth.
- **Kittens (6–12 months)**: 2–3 meals per day.
- **Adult cats**: 2 meals per day works for most. Some cats do well with
  a small third meal if portions are small.
- **Senior cats**: keep regular meals; some senior cats prefer smaller, more
  frequent portions.

## Wet vs dry food

- **Wet food** has higher moisture, which is helpful because cats often do
  not drink enough water on their own.
- **Dry food** is convenient and dental-friendly when designed for that
  purpose, but should not be the only source if the cat is prone to urinary
  problems.
- A mix of wet and dry, or all wet, is reasonable for most healthy adults.

## How much to feed

- Use the food's package guide as a starting point, then adjust to keep
  the cat at a healthy body condition (waist visible, ribs easy to feel).
- Indoor cats need fewer calories than outdoor cats of the same size.
- Treats should stay under ~10% of daily calories.

## What to avoid

- Cow's milk for adult cats — most are lactose intolerant and it causes
  diarrhoea.
- Raw fish as a regular item — it can deplete thiamine over time.
- Dog food — it lacks taurine, an essential amino acid for cats.
- Onion, garlic, and chives in any form (see toxic-food list).

## Hydration

- Multiple water sources around the home increase intake.
- Many cats prefer running water; pet fountains help.
- Sudden increase or decrease in drinking can signal kidney or thyroid
  problems and warrants a vet visit.

## When to call a vet

- A cat refusing food for more than 24 hours (cats can develop hepatic
  lipidosis quickly when not eating).
- Repeated vomiting beyond an occasional hairball.
- Weight loss without a diet change.
