# Dog vaccination schedule (typical)

Vaccination protocols are tailored by veterinarians to the individual dog
and local disease risk. The schedule below reflects widely recommended
**core vaccines** and common timing in the United States. Always confirm
with the dog's vet.

## Core vaccines (recommended for nearly all dogs)

- **DHPP / DAPP** — distemper, hepatitis (adenovirus), parainfluenza,
  parvovirus.
- **Rabies** — required by law in most jurisdictions.

## Non-core vaccines (depends on lifestyle and region)

- **Bordetella** (kennel cough) — for dogs in daycare, boarding, or grooming.
- **Leptospirosis** — for dogs exposed to wildlife, standing water, or rural
  environments.
- **Lyme disease** — in tick-heavy regions.
- **Canine influenza** — in some boarding/daycare facilities.

## Puppy schedule (typical)

- **6–8 weeks**: first DHPP.
- **10–12 weeks**: DHPP booster; start non-core (bordetella, lepto) if
  indicated.
- **14–16 weeks**: DHPP booster; **first rabies vaccination**.
- **16–20 weeks**: final DHPP booster.

Puppies should avoid contact with unvaccinated dogs and unknown public
spaces until at least 1–2 weeks after the final puppy DHPP booster.

## Adult dog schedule

- **1 year**: DHPP and rabies boosters one year after the puppy series.
- **Every 3 years (typical)**: DHPP and rabies, where local law allows
  3-year rabies. Some jurisdictions require yearly rabies.
- **Yearly**: bordetella, lepto, lyme, and canine flu when these are
  recommended for the dog's lifestyle.

## Common questions

- **Are titer tests an alternative?** Some core vaccines can be tracked
  with antibody titers, but rabies typically must follow the legally
  mandated schedule regardless of titer.
- **Vaccine reactions?** Mild lethargy or soreness for 24 hours is normal.
  Vomiting, facial swelling, hives, or trouble breathing are emergencies —
  contact a vet immediately.
- **Skipping vaccines for older dogs?** This is a vet decision. Senior dogs
  generally still need rabies and DHPP boosters unless a medical reason
  applies.

## Practical scheduling tips

- Add the next due date to a calendar at the time of each visit.
- Keep a written or digital record of vaccine dates and lot numbers — many
  boarding facilities require proof.
