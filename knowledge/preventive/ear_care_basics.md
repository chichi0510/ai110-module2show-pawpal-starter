# Routine ear care for dogs and cats

Healthy pet ears are usually pale pink and lightly waxy with no strong odour.
**Most pets do NOT need their ears cleaned on a fixed weekly schedule** — over-
cleaning irritates the ear canal and can push debris deeper.

## Cadence

- **Cats with healthy ears**: typically need cleaning only when wax becomes
  visible, often less than once a **month**.
- **Dogs with healthy upright ears**: a quick check every 2–4 **weeks** is
  enough; clean only when you can see waxy build-up.
- **Dogs with floppy ears, frequent swimmers, or breeds prone to ear
  infections** (e.g. cocker spaniels, basset hounds): cleaning every **week** to
  every other week reduces moisture-driven infections.

## How to clean safely

- Use a vet-approved ear cleaner; never use cotton swabs deep in the canal,
  hydrogen peroxide, or rubbing alcohol.
- Squeeze cleaner into the canal, gently massage the base of the ear, and let
  the pet shake before wiping the visible part with gauze or a soft cloth.

## When to call the vet

If you see any of these, stop home cleaning and call the **vet**:

- Strong yeasty or rotten odour, dark coffee-ground discharge, redness or
  swelling.
- Head shaking, ear scratching, or pawing at the ear lasting more than a day.
- Pain when the ear is touched, or visible blood in the ear canal.
